Stars

Stars are huge celestial bodies made mostly of hydrogen and helium
that produce light and heat from the churning nuclear forges inside their cores.

Galaxy

A galaxy is a huge collection of gas, dust, and billions of stars and their solar systems.
A galaxy is held together by gravity.
The above Galaxy is the Andromeda Galaxy (Closest Galaxy)

Black Hole

A black hole is a place in space where gravity pulls so much that even light can not get out.
This can happen when a star is dying.

Nebula

A nebula is a giant cloud of dust and gas in space.
Some nebulae (more than one nebula) come from the gas and dust thrown out by
the explosion of a dying star, such as a supernova.
The above is the Carina Nebula.

Space


Space, also known as outer space, is the near-vacuum between celestial bodies. It is where everything (all of the planets, stars, galaxies and other objects) is found. On Earth, space begins at the Kármán line (100 km above sea level). This is where Earth's atmosphere is said to stop and outer space begins. This is not a natural boundary but is a convention used by scientists and diplomats.

Space Exploration

Space exploration is the use of astronomy and space technology to explore outer space. While the exploration of space is carried out mainly by astronomers with telescopes, its physical exploration though is conducted both by unmanned robotic space probes and human spaceflight.
